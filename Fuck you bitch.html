Fuck you bitch

A sudden knock broke his dream. It was heavy knock but to his ears it seemed only a gentle tap of the back of the middle finger. He turned his side and put the pillow, pressing hard on his ears. The knock came back again and if he hadn’t had drink that night he would have heard a little noise that came after the knock. The knocks died down when he pressed the pillow in sort of way that one would press on ones mouth who was supposed to take the last breath. That what he murmured, under the blanket. Let me take my last breath. The knock died down, and when Roy recollected whole incident the next morning, he had only heard three gentle taps and nothing else. The scream before the thudd on the door after nothing. He was drunk alright.

The alarm broke his sleep, he put his thumb on the switch and turned it off. Then slid under the warmth of the blanket, the haze came back again.

The alarm sounded even louder the next time, and when he slid out his hand from the blanket, he tripped off it on the floor. The noise rang louder and louder. That was the hell of the choice for an alarm. The low pitch shrill that can wake up whole flat at once. He swayed his arm along the length of the bed, and only he could feel was the cold marble. He flinched his arm and pulled back into the blanket.

The haze came back, and then gone again when alarm didn’t stop. It was its job. He threw the blanket on the side and rose. It was five and seven he looked on the mobile. He turned on the torch and flashed on the floor. The light reflected over the shiny rim of the alarm. Why didn’t he threw that away.

Roy picked it up and switched it off. He swinged his arm around bed without actually losing the grip on the alarm. For moment he even felt that the smooth surface brushed over finger and the alarm was at the ends of his finger. And yet when the swing ended, it felt right back in his palm, the coldness.

He turned on the light and put it beside his childhood photoframe. The alarm was the only thing that remained of the relationship that he hadn’t crushed down or sent in a tapped box at Riya’s house. And how bad had that turned out, not exactly as he expected. Not even close.

Roy turned on the music on his phone and got into the bathroom. The booze hadn’t weared off and he could realize while watching his face in the mirror. The eyes were not red like last night, but still the pinkish hue was there to remind him of the drinks he had the last night.

The reason behind the booze he all remembered clearly. It was just what happened after that was blurred and hidden behind the haze. He picked up the brush from the shelf and put it into his mouth and sat on the toilet seat.

He tapped on the forehead for three times and even then unsatisfied he shaked his head to and fro. It didn’t help get off the hazy images of his head. Instead his head fell on his lap.

And when he woke up, he realized the toothbrush was barren and there wasn’t any paste on it. He removed the brush from his mouth and flushed down the toilet. He opened the shelf again and picked up the toothpaste and saw in the corner, an old toothbrush lying, RIYAS handwritten on it. He picked it and threw in the dustbin below.

This was the last thing of her. And ofcourse the bloody alarm. Reminds me the morning and Reminds me mornings with her. It rang in his mind until he shaked his head again.

The taste of the toothpaste seemed bitter on his tongue, but he didn’t care. He started moving, not dancing he didn’t like dancing, to the music. It was sort of like those kids who can’t pull their legs up or move their waist. They just up their arms in the air and jumps up and down without leaving the floor.

The toothpaste did work and the hazy images got off his mind. He rinsed his mouth, gargled for good two minutes and wiped his face.

God Damn you bastard He yelled a little. It was like whisper but in anger. He turned Honey Singh off his phone and got back to the bed.

He jumped on the bed and lied down on his back, his arms wide open, his legs barely touching the floor.

It was the daily schedule, after Riya had left her. Wake up early and get yourself running. It was under control for two weeks, the same fact he started doubting now. And suddenly yesterday evening when he saw Riya in the cafe taking sips from the coffee mug, it all got out of control. Fuck you bitch.

Roy got himself a bottle of vodka, from the store near the coffeehouse. Oh he wanted to say hello to her. Fuck you bitch. He reached the apartment and started drinking. Ordered Pizza, kept drinking. He didn’t even remember when the pizza boy came.

Halfway to the bottle he reached the balcony and lied there for good three hours, the slices settled him in and just when he started feeling little bit sober again. He got back in the apartment. He finished the half bottle in the next hour. Fuck you bitch.

Then it was haze, in his dreams Roy was with Riya for the vacation she always wanted. At Maldivs, her slaying the red bikini he got her. Him in the havaain shirt and cotten shorts below. And when she came back running from the sea all was gone and it was all haze and blurred and dark again. As if someone was tapping him on his ears.

There was no point get going on the running track. The bloody alarm didn’t think so. He opened his eyes and stared at the alarm seating beside his photoframe. It hadn’t lost its shine over the past two years. Same couldn’t be said … Fuck you bitch.

Roy got on his feet and hurried to his wardrobe. He threw one tshirt and a trackpant on his shoulder and got into the bathroom. When he came out, he was all ready. He put on his shoes. His hand stopped at the door knob and he remembered something he got back and tied the fitness band on his left wrist.

The band, Roy had bought on the next day when Riya left, it made him felt good when he was up and running the next day. A productive day he called and checked that on the calender. The same calender he was looking at now, thirteen days checked and add two more. Half a month. Fuck you bitch.

Roy turned the knob and opened the door. He gasped when he saw a figure which looked like a girl, her hairs were thick in the pool of the blood which strayed along the door. “Oh …” The blood was down to the door and had touched the rim around it.

For a moment he froze and few hazy pictures flashed in front of his eyes. They were real, the sound was real but they wiped off his eyes so quickly he couldn’t make what it meant.

He shut the door behind the body, his running shoes left the prints on the floor when he headed to the bed. He didn’t see it. Then his head started spinning, more than what it was before when he was boozed. He sat on the edge of the bed and pulled out his mobile.

Call the police, you fool. Call the police.

He didn’t. Instead he closed his eyes and lied down on the bed, with blood dripping from the shoes on the floor. The dream came back Riya was running towards him in the red bikini. She was laying her hands out in the air, like those old bollywood movies. Then as if the bikini was made of some paint the red color flowed down to her stomach, down to her thighs. And at that moment he forgot her face. What she looked like you fuck. Don’t you remember, you loved her. You asshole.

Roy jumped out of the bed and ran towards the door. He opened it with a thump and looked at the girl lying down on the floor. Oh fuck, that hair, how could you forget her hairs.

He knelt forward and turned her head in his lap. Her face was dripped with the blood, but he knew it was her. Oh Riya … Oh you fuck. Then suddenly he felt something on his arm, as if there was wind blowing on and off. The hairs moved on his arm. He turned her face and put a finger down below her nose. He felt the warm breath coming out of her nose. Oh you moron.

“Help ” Roy put Riya’s head with carefully on the floor and screamed. He ran back into the bedroom and picked his phone up. RIYA. The call history popped out like a needle but there was no time for it. He dialed 108 and a stream of tears came rolling down his cheeks.
